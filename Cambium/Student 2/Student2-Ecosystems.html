ECOSYSTEM / BIODIVERSITY

ECOSYSTEM

With biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components that interact with one another and their surroundings, ecosystems are intricate, interrelated systems. The size of these ecosystems varies from small ecosystems such as ponds or small woods to large ecosystems such as oceans or tropical rainforests.

Key features of ecosystems include:

Biodiversity:
ecosystems support a wide range of plant, animal and microbiological species, and each contributes to the maintenance of ecosystem services in its own way.

Energy flow:
ecosystems absorb, store, and distribute energy, which powers biological processes and supports life, through food webs and energy pyramids.

Cycle of nutrient:
nutrients, such as carbon, nitrogen and phosphorus, are taken from the environment by plant life that releases them back to the natural world or is recycled via decomposition.

Interactions:
Some of the ways in which organisms interact with one another and with their environment are predation, competition, and symbiosis. Ecosystems provide a wide range of ecological services that are crucial for human well being, including: Control of weather and climate The availability of clean water and air Creation of soils and nutrient cycling Pollination of natural vegetation and plant life Defense against natural disasters, including floods and erosion. But ecosystems are under increasing stress from human activities like pollution, habitat destruction, deforestation, and climate change, endangering their stability and well-being. The preservation of ecosystems and the services they offer depends on conservation initiatives and sustainable management techniques.

BIODIVERSITY

Biodiversity includes the diversity of life forms on Earth, including genetic variations, species differences and environmental variability.This is the result of billions of years of evolution, and it's necessary to keep ecosystems alive and functioning.

Genetically diverse:
the diversity of genes found in a species is considered to be genetically varied. It allows organisms to fight disease, adapt to changes in the environment and evolve as time passes. The likelihood of survival and viability in a species is increased by the greater diversity of its genes.

Species diversity:
This is a term that describes the range and abundance of individual species in an area or environment. It's covering everything from giant trees to charming megafauna and microscopic microbes. It is frequently associated with good ecosystems because of the high diversity of species, which provides resilience to ecological changes and protection from them.

Diversity:
the range of ecosystems in an area or on Earth as a whole is considered to be diverse. Some biological communities are supported by diverse habitats, such as wetlands, grasslands, forests and corals which provide a wide range of ecosystem services. The diversity of ecosystems plays an important role in the stability and resilience of natural systems, as well as protecting overall biodiversity.

In order to support ecosystem functions and services, biodiversity has a key role to play, including:
Control of air quality and climate soil and water purification supplying food, medication, and other necessities Pollination of natural vegetation and crops Management of illnesses and pests Encouragement of artistic and leisure pursuits

However, human actions like habitat destruction, overuse of natural resources, pollution, and climate change are putting biodiversity in jeopardy. Protecting and restoring biodiversity as well as guaranteeing the long-term survival of species and ecosystems depend on conservation activities such as protected areas, habitat restoration, sustainable land management, and community-based conservation programmes.
